Evidence #1: Course Concept Impact

The Mechanics of Language

Literary devices are far more than rules designed for simple identification; they serve as the vital architecture required to breathe life into a text. Studying these elements in depth has altered my understanding of narrative creation, teaching me how to look beneath surface-level words to discover structural intent.

Metaphors, similes, foreshadowing, symbolism, personification, and sensory imagery each act as specific layers that shape a piece of writing. These frameworks have helped change how I analyze literature.

How It Impacted My Writing

Entering this course, literary elements felt like static definitions reserved strictly for vocabulary tests, and I rarely utilized them with actual intent within my own drafting phases. However, developing an understanding of their functions has updated my writing strategy.

Instead of simply summarizing actions, I now try to structure my short stories and paragraph writing with solid thematic anchors. Integrating subtle foreshadowing or clean symbolism turns simple write-ups into much more cohesive and engaging paragraphs.
