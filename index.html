About The Course

Grade 9 English focuses on building foundational high school skills in reading, writing, and critical thinking. Throughout the year, the curriculum emphasizes analyzing various literary genres, including novels, short stories, and drama, while developing structured paragraph and essay-writing techniques. Students also practice oral communication through discussions and presentations to build confidence in expressing their ideas.

My Experience

Coming into Grade 9, the jump to high school English felt like a big step. This course challenged me to look past just the plot of a story and figure out why an author wrote it. My biggest achievement this year was improving my analytical skills. I learned how to back up my arguments with solid evidence from the text instead of just guessing. While speaking in front of the class was tough at first, I'm proud of how much more confident I've become in sharing my perspective.

About Me

Hello! My name is Aayush, and I am a Grade 9 student at Chinguacousy Secondary. This website is the final summative project for my English course, representing a semester of exploring literature, analyzing media, and developing my voice as a writer. Throughout this year, we dove into complex themes like identity and story-telling through texts like Motorcycles and Sweetgrass. I hope you enjoy exploring my growth as a student this term!

Impact

Literary devices aren't decorative, they're structural. Studying literature taught me to ask not what a text says, but how it creates meaning. Metaphor, symbolism, personification, imagery: each one layers depth beneath the surface. Those skills transferred everywhere: into how I read, write, and communicate. Once you understand how language is built, you can't unsee it.
