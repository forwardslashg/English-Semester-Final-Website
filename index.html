About The Course

Grade 9 English focuses on building foundational high school skills in reading, writing, and critical thinking. Throughout the year, the curriculum emphasizes analyzing various literary genres, including novels, short stories, and drama, while developing structured paragraph and essay-writing techniques. Students also practice oral communication through discussions and presentations to build confidence in expressing their ideas.

My Experience

Coming into Grade 9, the jump to high school English felt like a big step. This course challenged me to look past just the plot of a story and figure out why an author wrote it. My biggest achievement this year was improving my analytical skills. I learned how to back up my arguments with solid evidence from the text instead of just guessing. While speaking in front of the class was tough at first, I'm proud of how much more confident I've become in sharing my perspective.

Impact

Literary devices aren't decorative, they're structural. A metaphor forces two ideas to collide. Foreshadowing builds investment before events occur. Irony creates distance that exposes, amuses, or deepens. Studying literature meant seeing language as deliberate choices, every word, image, and structure communicating beyond its literal meaning. I stopped asking what a text says and started asking how it creates meaning. These skills transferred everywhere: metaphors in political speeches, symbolism in advertising, irony in everyday conversation. I became a more critical reader and a more intentional writer. This learning shifted how I approach all communication, not just literature. I now notice metaphors in political speeches, symbolism in advertising, and irony in everyday conversation. It also made me a more intentional writer. Understanding devices gave me a toolkit: I could use repetition to build emphasis, contrast to highlight difference, or extended metaphor to make an abstract idea concrete and memorable.
