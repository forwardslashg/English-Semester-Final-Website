Core Transferable Skills

1. Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

Critical thinking is a skill I consistently apply across both my academic endeavors and my personal life. Inside the classroom, this ability allows me to look beyond surface-level facts, whether I am analyzing the underlying motives behind a character's actions or evaluating the validity of different sources for a research project.

Rather than just memorizing data, I actively question, dissect, and synthesize information, making it an indispensable tool for my overall intellectual development.

Classroom Application: Text & Context Analysis

2. Digital Literacy

Digital literacy is a fundamental competence, encompassing everything from technical proficiency to responsible digital citizenship. In my academic work, this skill is on display daily as I navigate software ecosystems, manage file directories, and write the custom code used to build this online portfolio.

It also means knowing how to efficiently research, filter out online misinformation, and evaluate digital sources for accuracy to keep an organized, professional workflow.

Real-World Use: Web Architecture & Research
